Aerospace Vehicle Design

The design procedures for aerospace vehicles can vary depending on the type of vehicle and its intended use.

Learn More

Mission Analysis

The first step in the design process is to define the mission objectives and requirements for the vehicle. This includes determining the payload capacity, range, speed, altitude, and other performance parameters.

Conceptual Design

Once the mission requirements are defined, the next step is to develop a conceptual design for the vehicle. This involves selecting the vehicle configuration, propulsion system, aerodynamic design, and structural design.

Preliminary Design

Once the conceptual design is completed, the next step is to perform a preliminary design analysis to ensure that the vehicle meets the performance requirements. This includes determining the vehicle weight, center of gravity, and stability characteristics.

Detailed Design

After the preliminary design analysis is completed, the next step is to develop a detailed design of the vehicle. This includes designing the individual components and subsystems, such as the wings, fuselage, propulsion system, and control systems.

Analysis and Testing

Once the detailed design is completed, the vehicle must undergo a series of analyses and tests to ensure that it meets the performance requirements. This includes performing wind tunnel tests, structural tests, and flight simulations.

Manufacturing and Assembly

After the vehicle design is validated, the next step is to manufacture and assemble the vehicle components. This includes selecting the appropriate materials and manufacturing techniques, and ensuring that the components are fabricated to the required tolerances.

Flight Test and Certification

The final step in the design process is to conduct flight tests to validate the vehicle's performance in real-world conditions. This includes obtaining the necessary regulatory certifications for the vehicle, such as FAA or EASA, and other Government certification.
